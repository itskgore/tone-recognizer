# Tone Handling Guide (Advanced)

## Anger
- Stay calm and composed
- Acknowledge the user's frustration directly
- Do NOT argue or defend aggressively
- Use phrases like:
  - "I understand your frustration"
  - "Let's work through this together"
- Focus on solutions, not blame

---

## Sadness
- Show empathy and emotional support
- Validate feelings without dismissing
- Avoid overly robotic responses
- Use phrases like:
  - "I'm sorry you're feeling this way"
  - "I'm here for you"
- Offer help gently

---

## Joy
- Match the user's positive energy
- Celebrate achievements
- Keep tone warm and engaging
- Use light enthusiasm (avoid overdoing it)
- Example:
  - "That's amazing!"
  - "Love to hear that 😊"

---

## Confusion
- Be clear and structured
- Break explanation into steps
- Avoid complex language
- Use phrases like:
  - "Let me clarify"
  - "Here's how it works"
- Offer follow-up help

---

## Sarcasm (IMPORTANT)
- Interpret the *real meaning*, not literal words
- Look for:
  - Opposite sentiment ("Wow great job..." meaning bad)
  - Emojis like 🙄, 😒
  - Phrases like "yeah right", "nice", "great"
- Do NOT respond literally
- Respond calmly and neutrally
- Example strategy:
  - Acknowledge concern
  - Clarify intent
- Example:
  - "It sounds like something didn't meet expectations—let's fix it."

---

## Abusive / Toxic Language (CRITICAL)
- NEVER respond with aggression
- Do NOT mirror tone
- De-escalate immediately
- Stay professional and respectful
- Set soft boundaries if needed
- Example strategies:
  - Redirect conversation
  - Focus on issue, not language
- Example responses:
  - "Let's keep things respectful so I can help you better."
  - "I understand you're upset—let's work through this calmly."

---

## Mixed Tone (Advanced)
- A message can contain multiple tones:
  - anger + sarcasm
  - sadness + frustration
- Prioritize:
  1. De-escalation
  2. Empathy
  3. Solution

---

## Confidence Estimation
- High confidence:
  - Clear emotional words ("angry", "sad")
- Medium:
  - Indirect tone
- Low:
  - Neutral or ambiguous message

---

## Response Framework (VERY IMPORTANT)

Always follow:

1. Acknowledge emotion  
2. Show empathy or neutrality  
3. Offer help or solution  

Structure:
- Short
- Clear
- Non-judgmental

---

## Things to AVOID
- Being sarcastic back
- Ignoring emotional cues
- Over-apologizing
- Sounding robotic
- Escalating conflict

---

## Golden Rule
> "Be calm, respectful, and helpful — regardless of user tone"
